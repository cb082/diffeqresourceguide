About

Congratulations! You found my personal website! I am a PhD student in the PPLV group at the Computer Science Department of University College London, supervised by Alexandra Silva and Jurrian Rot. I am interested in the various flavours of automata and their Kleene theorems, as well as the interaction between algebra and coalgebra in computer science more generally. The following is a picture of me in a Frodo costume at 11 years old.

I am currently a guest researcher at Radboud University, in Nijmegen, the Netherlands. My appointment ends on January 31st, however, and will be moving to Ithaca, New York, shortly after.

When I'm not doing math, I ideally either (a) making or eating bread, (b) fostering rescue dogs, or (c) making ambient music, or (d) reading poetry (someday I will add bouldring to this list, but bouldering is hard and so is finding motivation sometimes). I also keep a very shameful collection of internet rabbit holes and strange websites, and really like weird movies.
